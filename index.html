Trauma recovery

Your story. Your strength.

Trauma doesn’t define you, but it can shape how you move through the world. Healing isn’t about erasing the past; it’s about reclaiming your future.

Trauma isn’t measured by what happened — it’s measured by its impact. That impact can show up as anxiety, panic, numbness, difficulty trusting, perfectionism, feeling stuck, or struggling in relationships. As a specialist in somatic-based trauma therapies like EMDR and TRM, I help people process and release trauma in ways that feel safe, effective, and lasting.

Treatment modalities

Traumatic experiences can reorganize your nervous system — what we often call being “triggered” — in ways that sit outside conscious control, which is why talk therapy alone is often not enough. I may use one or a combination of these approaches:

  • Attachment-Focused EMDR (AF-EMDR), Trauma Resiliency Model (TRM), and Cognitive Processing Therapy (CPT) — integrated into complex psychotherapy, as brief focused work, or as an adjunct to care with another therapist.
  • Brief psychotherapy — often 8–12 sessions, one issue, directive and solution-focused.
  • Complex psychotherapy — deeper work when multiple interrelated issues need time and patience.
  • “Life support” — ongoing self-care and wellness-focused therapy (often not insurance-covered).
